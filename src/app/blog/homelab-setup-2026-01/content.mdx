# Homelab Setup as of Jan 30th, 2026

It's almost been a year since my last update on what's changed with my Homelab. Time to update that.
I knew once I started this stuff I'd never get out of it. 

You'll notice a lot of this has become "Ahh I really _need_ this" to "Hey, _can_ I do this?" and then impulsively purchase and do w/e the idea of the day is. But honestly learning more than what I thought out of it and having too much fun in the process.

## Hardware
**Server**: ZimaBlade 7700
No changes and no complaints

**PoE Switch**: [TP-Link TL-SG1008P](https://www.tp-link.com/us/business-networking/unmanaged-switch/tl-sg1008p/)
Already looking to expand...

*Now to the fun stuff*

**Cameras**:  [Reolink RLC-520A ](https://www.tp-link.com/us/business-networking/unmanaged-switch/tl-sg1008p/)
These cameras are better than most businesses and schools. PoE and connected directly to home assistant (more below). Set up on my front porch and back porch. [Frigate](https://docs.frigate.video/) to serve for object detection, truly amazing. *chefs kiss*

**Mesh WiFi**: [Motorola MH7023 ](https://www.newegg.com/motorola-mh7023-10-mesh-wifi-system/p/N82E16833301070)
Since I've started working on a laptop, I have the freedom to work on my back porch, but my poor AT&T router doesn't reach from my office to there. So rather than upgrade or move the router, let's just buy a whole mesh system for the house. 

Entirely way too overkill for the house, but it's cool right?

**Moisture Meter:** [Ecowitt GW1000](https://www.ecowitt.com/shop/goodsDetail/16#) with [WH51 Wireless Soil Moisture Meter](https://shop.ecowitt.com/products/wh51)
Because why not? It's in my garden and keeps me on track of watering. I've already got an automatic watering nozzle - thingy. But why not?

#### 3. Frigate NVR
Local AI-powered camera recording and object detection for the cameras. Basically my own ring cameras, but I get to control it. Honestly one of the biggest draws for me getting my own cameras was to see how easy it was to setup object detection. I don't live in a bad part of town, moreso, I just wanted to have AI object detection on a camera... so I did it.

**Features**:
  - AI object detection (person, car, packages, etc)
  - Motion-based recording 
  - Snapshots enabled for review
  
**Downside:** When the model is recording and detecting an object CPU spikes up to 60%+ from it's usual less than 5% üò¨. I looked into maybe [coral](https://www.coral.ai/products/) has a solution for this? Decided to leave it as it is, but something to note for later.

#### 4. Glance Dashboard
Unified home lab dashboard and my (pseudo) service monitoring. I say pseudo because previously I used Uptime Kuma, which is a proper program for monitoring, but I really didn't see a need for it for what I'm using. So I'll use the small widget that Glance provides for it. It's has become my homepage on my browsers, just a quick look (or glance) at everything before start surfing.

**Features**:
  - Service status monitoring with visual indicators
  - Weather widget for local conditions
  - Stock ticker monitoring
  - Search functionality with custom shortcuts
  - Quick links to all homelab services
  - Feed from hackernew's top articles
